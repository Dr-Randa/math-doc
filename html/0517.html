Abstract

In this paper, we study the continued fraction y⁢(s,r) which satisfies the equation y⁢(s,r)⁢y⁢(s+2⁢r,r)=(s+1)⁢(s+2⁢r-1) for r>12. This continued fraction is a generalization of the Brouncker’s continued fraction b⁢(s). We extend the formulas for the first and the second logarithmic derivatives of b⁢(s) to the case of y⁢(s,r). The asymptotic series for y⁢(s,r) at ∞ are also studied. The generalizations of some Ramanujan’s formulas are presented.

Keywords: Brouncker’s continued fraction, Ramanujan’s formula, Asymptotic series, Functional equations. Primary 11A55 Secondary 11J70, 30B70.

1 Introduction

The Brouncker’s continued fraction b⁢(s)=s+Kn=1∞⁡((2⁢n-1)22⁢s) still attracts the attention of researchers due to its role in the theory of orthogonal polynomials and its relations to the Gamma and Beta functions (see [], []–[]). Recall the following theorem of Brouncker describing the properties of b⁢(s) (see [], p. 145, Theorem 3.16).

Theorem 1.1 (Brouncker)

Let b⁢(s) be a function on (0,+∞) satisfying the functional equation b⁢(s)⁢b⁢(s+2)=(s+1)2 and the inequality s<b⁢(s) for s>C, where C is some constant. Then

b⁢(s)=(s+1)⁢∏n=1∞(s+4⁢n-3)⁢(s+4⁢n+1)(s+4⁢n-1)2=s+Kn=1∞⁡((2⁢n-1)22⁢s)

for every positive s.

Ramanujan discovered the formula, expressing the Brouncker’s continued fraction in terms of the Gamma function (see [], p. 153, Theorem 3.25).

2 Functional equations for logarithmic derivatives of y⁢(s,r)

Let us recall the following statement, which will be used later (see [], p. 152, Lemma 3.23).

Lemma 2.1

Let g⁢(s) be a monotonic function on (0,∞), vanishing at infinity, and a>0 be a positive number. Then the functional equation f⁢(s)+f⁢(s+a)=g⁢(s) has a unique solution, vanishing at infinity, given by the formula

f⁢(s)=∑n=0∞(-1)n⁢g⁢(s+n⁢a).

Let us prove two following statements for the first and the second logarithmic derivatives of y⁢(s,r).

Lemma 2.2

The functional equation

f⁢(s,r)+f⁢(s+2⁢r,r)=1s+1+1s+2⁢r-1=2⁢(s+r)(s+1)⁢(s+2⁢r-1) 5

has a unique solution, satisfying lims→∞⁡f⁢(s,r)=0, which is

f⁢(s,r)=∂∂⁡s⁢(ln⁡y)⁢(s,r).

Proof. The equality y⁢(s,r)⁢y⁢(s+2⁢r,r)=(s+1)⁢(s+2⁢r-1) implies

ln⁡(y⁢(s,r)⁢y⁢(s+2⁢r,r))=ln⁡((s+1)⁢(s+2⁢r-1));
lny(s,r)+lny(s+2r,r)=ln(s+1)+ln(s+2r-1)).

Differentiating by s, we obtain

∂∂⁡s⁢y⁢(s,r)y⁢(s,r)+∂∂⁡s⁢y⁢(s+2⁢r,r)y⁢(s+2⁢r,r)=1s+1+1s+2⁢r-1. 6

The function f⁢(s)=∂∂⁡s⁢yy⁢(s,r) satisfy the conditions of Lemma 1 with a=2⁢r and g⁢(s)=2⁢(s+r)(s+1)⁢(s+2⁢r-1). Applying Lemma 1, we complete the proof. □

Let us examine two equations:

f1⁢(s,r)+f1⁢(s+2⁢r,r)=1s+1 7

and

f2⁢(s,r)+f2⁢(s+2⁢r,r)=1s+2⁢r-1. 8

Both of them satisfy the conditions of Lemma 1 with a=2⁢r, g⁢(s)=1s+1 and g⁢(s)=1s+2⁢r-1, respectively. So, applying Lemma 1, we obtain, that the solution f1⁢(s,r) of equation (7) which satisfies lims→∞⁡f1⁢(s,r)=0 is unique. The solution f2⁢(s,r) of equation (8) which satisfies lims→∞⁡f2⁢(s,r)=0 is also unique. Since their sum f1⁢(s,r)+f2⁢(s,r) satisfies equation (5), we have from Lemma 2, that

∂∂⁡s⁢(ln⁡y)⁢(s,r)=f1⁢(s,r)+f2⁢(s,r),

where f1⁢(s,r) and f2⁢(s,r) are the solutions of (7) and (8), respectively, vanishing as s→+∞.

Lemma 2.3

The functional equation

f⁢(s,r)+f⁢(s+2⁢r,r)=-1(s+1)2-1(s+2⁢r-1)2 9

has a unique solution, satisfying lims→∞⁡f⁢(s,r)=0, which is

f⁢(s,r)=∂2∂2⁡s⁢(ln⁡y)⁢(s,r).

Proof. Differentiate equation (6) once again by s:

∂∂⁡s⁢(∂∂⁡s⁢y⁢(s,r)y⁢(s,r))+∂∂⁡s⁢(∂∂⁡s⁢y⁢(s+2⁢r,r)y⁢(s+2⁢r,r))=-1(s+1)2-1(s+2⁢r-1)2.

Applying Lemma 1 with a=2⁢r and g⁢(s)=-1(s+1)2-1(s+2⁢r-1)2, we complete the proof. □

Repeating the above reasoning, we obtain that

-∂2∂2⁡s⁢(ln⁡y)⁢(s,r)=h1⁢(s,r)+h2⁢(s,r), 10

where h1⁢(s) is the unique solution of the equation

h1⁢(s,r)+h1⁢(s+2⁢r,r)=1(s+1)2, 11

satisfying lims→∞⁡h1⁢(s,r)=0 and h2⁢(s) is the unique solution of the equation

h2⁢(s,r)+h2⁢(s+2⁢r,r)=1(s+2⁢r-1)2, 12

satisfying lims→∞⁡h2⁢(s,r)=0.
